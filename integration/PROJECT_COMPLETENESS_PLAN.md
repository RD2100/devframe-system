## 0. Boundary

This document is the parent-level plan for `D:\devframe-system`.

It does not dispatch submodule agents, does not modify submodule business code,
does not update submodule pins, does not run real external runtime, and does
not authorize real Zotero, Obsidian, RAG, WriteLab, MiniApp, browser/CDP, cloud,
or private paper content.

Submodules are allowed to self-iterate in their own threads. The parent repo
work here is to define standards, collect verified facts, maintain architecture
and contract alignment, and produce Go/No-Go decisions after submodule evidence
returns.

## 1. Current Parent Goal

`devframe-system` should become a practical coordination and verification
framework, not a perfect platform.

The parent repo must provide:

- a single reality inventory;
- clear module boundaries;
- integration contracts;
- evidence and review standards;
- negative matrix and fake-green prevention;
- read-only CI/canary plan;
- risk register and phase gates;
- final Master Plan report;
- pin readiness judgement after submodule evidence is independently reviewed.

The parent repo must not claim that candidate, dry-run, test summary, reviewer
pack, ZIP validation, or dispatch success equals final acceptance.

## 2. Active Assumptions

- `dev-frame-opencode`, `test-frame`, and `agent-acceptance` are already
  self-iterating.
- `devframe-control-plane` stays frozen except for interface observation.
- The main coordinator still owns submodule dispatch, report receipt,
  blocker handling, and parent pin decisions.
- This planning role owns parent-level completeness, standards, architecture,
  plan integration, and later evidence alignment.
- Unknown facts must be marked `UNKNOWN`, `GAP`, `HUMAN_REQUIRED`, or
  `WAITING_FOR_SUBMODULE_REPORT`, not guessed.

## 3. Evidence Vocabulary

Use these terms consistently:

- `verified`: checked from disk, command output, report, or exact artifact path.
- `observed`: visible in current worktree but not independently accepted.
- `candidate`: useful offline/dry-run evidence, not final readiness.
- `blocked`: cannot continue without missing prerequisite or human decision.
- `failed`: check ran and failed.
- `human_required`: requires human approval or real external/private data.
- `waiting_for_submodule_report`: parent is waiting for a self-iterating module.
- `final_ready`: only after independent review and explicit final acceptance.
